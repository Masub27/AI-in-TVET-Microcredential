# Micro-Credential Definition

## Purpose of This Document
This document explains the meaning of micro-credentials and provides the theoretical basis for the AI-in-TVET Microcredential project.

## UNESCO Perspective
According to UNESCO, micro-credentials are a flexible way to recognize knowledge, skills, and competences. They are usually focused on a specific set of learning outcomes and are completed in a shorter period of time than traditional qualifications.

UNESCO describes a micro-credential as:

- A record of focused learning achievement.
- Evidence of what the learner knows, understands, or can do.
- Based on assessment against clearly defined standards.
- Awarded by a trusted provider.
- Valuable as a standalone credential.
- Possible to combine with other micro-credentials or larger qualifications.
- Supported by relevant quality assurance.

## European Union Perspective
The European approach defines a micro-credential as a record of learning outcomes that a learner has acquired after a small volume of learning. These learning outcomes must be assessed against transparent and clearly defined standards.

The EU approach also explains that micro-credentials:

- Are owned by the learner.
- Can be shared.
- Are portable.
- May be standalone.
- May be combined into larger credentials.
- Should be supported by quality assurance.

## Definition Used in This Project
For this project, a micro-credential means:

> A short, focused, and assessed learning experience that records what a learner knows, understands, or can do in a specific area of AI-supported teaching workflow creation.

## Project Context
This micro-credential focuses on AI-supported teaching workflow creation in TVET. Learners will use GitHub, VS Code, GitHub Copilot, LiaScript, and the BMAD Method to create online and offline educational materials.

## Key Features of This Micro-Credential
This micro-credential includes:

1. Clear learning outcomes.
2. A small and focused learning volume.
3. Practical learning tasks.
4. Assessment based on transparent criteria.
5. Evidence of learner achievement.
6. Online and offline learning outputs.
7. A certificate or badge template.
8. Quality assurance through checklists and review.

## Why This Micro-Credential Is Important
This micro-credential supports flexible learning in TVET. It helps teachers, trainers, and students develop practical skills for creating digital learning materials with AI-supported tools.

It is also useful because it connects educational technology, AI, teaching design, and online/offline delivery in one structured learning package.
