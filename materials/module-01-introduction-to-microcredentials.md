# Module 1: Introduction to Micro-Credentials

## Module Overview
This module introduces the concept of micro-credentials and explains their importance in modern education, lifelong learning, and Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET).

Micro-credentials are useful because they recognise focused learning achievements in a short and flexible way. They can support learners, teachers, trainers, and workers who need specific skills for education or employment.

## Module Aim
The aim of this module is to help learners understand what micro-credentials are and how they can be used in TVET and AI-supported teaching workflows.

## Learning Outcomes
After completing this module, learners will be able to:

1. Define micro-credentials in simple words.
2. Explain the difference between micro-credentials and traditional qualifications.
3. Describe why micro-credentials are useful for flexible learning.
4. Identify important elements of a micro-credential.
5. Connect micro-credentials with TVET and AI-supported teaching workflows.

## Key Concept

A micro-credential is a short and focused learning record. It shows that a learner has achieved specific learning outcomes after completing a small learning experience.

In this project, a micro-credential means:

> A short, focused, and assessed learning experience that records what a learner knows, understands, or can do in the area of AI-supported teaching workflow creation.

## Why Micro-Credentials Are Important

Micro-credentials are important because they:

- Support flexible learning.
- Focus on specific skills and competences.
- Can be completed in a shorter time than traditional qualifications.
- Help learners show evidence of achievement.
- Can support lifelong learning.
- Can be useful for employment and professional development.
- Can be combined with other micro-credentials or larger qualifications.

## Micro-Credentials in TVET

In TVET, micro-credentials can support practical skill development. They can help learners gain specific competences needed for teaching, training, workplace learning, and digital education.

For example, a TVET learner or teacher may complete a micro-credential on:

- Creating digital learning materials.
- Using AI tools for lesson planning.
- Designing online and offline learning activities.
- Building a GitHub-based teaching workflow.
- Using LiaScript for interactive learning content.

## Micro-Credentials and AI-in-TVET

This micro-credential focuses on AI-supported teaching workflow creation.

Learners will use:

- GitHub for storing and sharing learning materials.
- VS Code for editing and organizing files.
- GitHub Copilot or AI support for content creation.
- LiaScript for online learning materials.
- BMAD Method for structured workflow planning.
